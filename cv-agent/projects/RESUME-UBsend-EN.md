# Project Experience

Start date (month/year): 11/2020
End date (month/year): 02/2021

## Client

Client name: UBsend A/S
Project name: UBsend

UBsend is a freight broker owned by the BestSeller group, which operates a range of clothing stores both physically and online in several countries. The platform serves as a unified integration point that connects online stores with local freight companies across national borders. This enables stores to plan and manage freight shipments to many different countries through a single solution, instead of having to integrate with each individual freight company. Although UBsend is part of BestSeller, the platform was at the time primarily used by external companies, including a larger German clothing store.

Customers needed to ship packages throughout Europe and from several different countries, which required integration with multiple last mile providers in each country. The platform's stability was crucial to ensure that customers could trust their packages would be delivered on time.

The project was in a situation where the development staff had gone from 10+ employees down to just one remaining developer after a major staff reduction. There was a need to quickly stabilize the platform and expand it with new integrations in order to serve customers across several European markets.

## Developer's Role

Role name: Software Developer (Backend)

Jonas Nielsen worked in a small team consisting of four developers and a Product Owner, where he joined along with two other new hires after a major staff reduction. The only remaining developer from the original team of 10+ did not have a formal lead role, so Jonas worked very independently with great responsibility for his own codebase.

Jonas' primary task was to optimize and build new microservices responsible for integrations with external partners. The new microservices were built in Java, while maintenance of existing legacy components required C# and .NET. Each microservice was responsible for integration with specific freight companies or domains, which isolated errors and made the system more robust.

One of Jonas' most significant achievements was solving a critical Out-of-Memory problem that had remained unsolved by the previous developer. Jonas analyzed the codebase and identified the cause of the problem, after which he implemented a solution that reduced memory consumption to 1/13,250 of the original level. This optimization ensured the platform's stability and enabled continued operation without downtime.

Jonas took responsibility for developing two new integrations with external freight companies from scratch. These integrations had to handle different data formats and APIs, as each freight company has their own way of receiving and processing shipment data. The integrations expanded the platform's coverage to more European countries and gave customers more options for last mile delivery.

As a result of Jonas' efforts, the platform's stability was secured, and the integrations expanded UBsend's capabilities to serve customers across several European markets.

The team worked with an architecture based on microservices, where different services had responsibility for specific domains. Jonas contributed to keeping the architecture simple and maintainable despite the limited staffing.
